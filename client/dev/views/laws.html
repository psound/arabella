LAWS

Each state has its own distracted driving laws. Below is our summary of your state’s distracted driving laws, fines and penalties. Remember to check your state’s Web site for the most up-to-date information.
CONNECTICUT

Ban on all cell phone use (handheld and hands-free) for novice drivers (those under the age of 18).

1st offense: 30 day license suspension, $125 license restoration fee plus court fines
2nd offense: $250 fine, 6 month suspension or until the teen driver reaches the age of 18, whichever is longer, $125 license restoration fee, plus court fines.

Ban on texting for drivers of all ages.

1st offense: $100
2nd offense: $150
3rd offense: $200

Handheld ban for drivers of all ages.

1st offense: $125
2nd offense: $250
3rd offense: $400
MASSACHUSETTS

Ban on texting for drivers of all ages.

1st offense: $100
2nd offense: $250
3rd offense: $500

Ban on all cell phone use (handheld and hands-free) for novice drivers (under the age of 18 with a learner’s permit or provisional license).

1st offense: $100 fine, 60-day license suspension, mandatory driving course
2nd offense:: $250 fine, 180-day license suspension
3rd offense:: $500 fine, 1-year license suspension
NEW HAMPSHIRE

Ban on all cell phone use (handheld and hands-free) for novice drivers (those under the age of 18).

1st offense: $50 fine
2nd offense: $50 fine
3rd offense: $100 fine, license suspended until age 18

Handheld ban for drivers of all ages

1st offense: $100 fine
2nd offense: $250 fine
3rd offense: $500 fine
RHODE ISLAND

Ban on all cell phone use (handheld and hands-free) for novice drivers (those under the age of 18).

1st offense: $50 fine
2nd offense: $50 fine
3rd offense: $100 fine, license suspended until age 18

Ban on texting for drivers of all ages.

1st offense: $85 fine
2nd offense: $100 fine
3rd offense: $125 fine
